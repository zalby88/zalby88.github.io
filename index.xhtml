ABOUT ME

Just a self-motivated Software Engineer, looking for a solid, yet dynamic career. Able to hold different positions (Frontend developer, Integration/Middleware Engineer, Tester) thanks to a broad academic preparation and to the habit to work meticulously in contexts requiring self-directed learning skills.

My commitment to study, combined with the challenging university program, helped me to develop strong mathematical and analytical skills.
By taking part to the most of team-working projects that were available at my university, I learnt to develop complete and well-documented software while becoming familiar with JAVA, C, and C++ programming languages. Moreover my passion for technology pushed me to learn other languages on my own, such as Python or DBMSs like MySQL and MongoDB.

A month after my graduation I had been hired by Reply, one of the leading Consulting and System Integration companies in Italy. Immediately I had been introduced to one of its main customers (Unicredit Business Integrated Solutions). By working inside the customer office in Verona, I had to deal with mixed teams, belonging to different external consulting companies. In this extremely competitive situation I had to learn as fast as possible the technologies adopted by the bank IT division (J2EE for most) in order to get ready to work on them while becoming familiar, for the fist time, to deal with a multilayered enterprise application. Also, I had often been asked to do regression tests, independently, on features developed by others as well as fix any bugs encountered during the process. This strongly challenged my ability in reverse engineering.

From March 2015 I had been moved to UBIS main office in Milano where I had been engaged in a frontend overhaul project for the entire Internet Banking section. As part of that, mastering IBM Websphere Portal technologies proved to be essential for delivering the new web application with the highest quality standards. Also, working in a Scrum-based team, gave me familiarity with the Agile approach.

From March 2017 I have finally moved to the United Kingdom and started to work for BGL Group. Here I learnt most of the Extreme Programming methodologies like Test Driven Development and Pair/Mob Programming. By being involved in a full re-engineering of the Policy Management web journey, I have integrated many of existing data layer procedures into a brand new, RESTful based, middleware layer. This one is intended to be exposed as a set of public APIs.
